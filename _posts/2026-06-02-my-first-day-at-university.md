My first day at university was exciting, but it was also confusing because everything was new. I had to understand the campus, classrooms, timetable, teachers, and new classmates. Before joining university, I had many expectations about university life. I thought it would be very different from college, with a large environment and many activities. When I arrived, I found that our campus is mostly focused on studies and academic work.
Students usually come to attend lectures, complete assignments, prepare for quizzes, and participate in lab sessions. The campus is comparatively small, and there are limited places for students to sit and relax. There is also only a small dhaba for food. At first, this environment felt different from what I had imagined, but slowly I understood that every university has its own routine and culture.
The first day was important because it was the beginning of a new stage in my life. I felt nervous while meeting new people, but after some days I became comfortable with my classmates and teachers. This experience taught me that adjustment takes time. I did not understand everything on the first day, but I slowly learned how university life works.
