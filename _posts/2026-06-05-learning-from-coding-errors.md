Coding errors were one of the most frustrating parts of my Programming Fundamentals learning experience. Sometimes a missing bracket, a spelling mistake, a wrong condition, or a small syntax error stopped the whole program from running. In the beginning, I did not understand why a small mistake could create such a big problem.
Whenever my program gave the wrong output, I had to check the code line by line. This process was slow, but it taught me patience. I learned that debugging is an important part of programming. Errors are not only problems; they are clues that help us find what is wrong in our logic or code.
With practice, I became more careful while writing programs. I started checking variable names, brackets, conditions, and output statements before running the code. I also learned that it is better to solve a program in small steps instead of writing everything at once.
This experience improved my attention to detail and logical thinking. It also taught me not to give up quickly when something does not work. These lessons later became useful during our Machine Learning projects, where technical problems required even more patience and teamwork.
