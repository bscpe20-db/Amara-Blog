During Programming Fundamentals, my group worked on a project called Heart Attack Risk Prediction using Machine Learning. This was one of the most meaningful experiences of my first semester because it connected programming with a real-world health problem. We used a health-related dataset, studied different factors, trained a Machine Learning model, prepared graphs, and wrote a project report.
Working on this project helped me understand that coding can be used for more than simple programs. Technology can help analyze data and support decision-making in important fields such as healthcare. Our project focused on predicting heart attack risk based on available data. We tried to explain our work in a simple and understandable way through graphs, results, and documentation.
Dr. Bilal Ahmad has expertise in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Deep Learning model training. He often encourages students to choose meaningful problems that have value in the real world. His focus on medical datasets helped me understand why reliable data is important when working on health-related projects.
I also learned that teamwork is important in projects. We had to divide tasks, understand the report, prepare the model, and discuss our results together. This project increased my interest in Machine Learning and prepared us for the exhibition.
For more information about his research background, I included [Dr. Bilal Ahmad’s Google Scholar profile](https://scholar.google.com.au/citations?user=8nZ0jVkAAAAJ&hl=en).
