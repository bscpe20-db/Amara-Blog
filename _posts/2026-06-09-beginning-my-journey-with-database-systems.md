In my second semester, I started studying Database Systems. This course helped me understand how data is stored, organized, and managed. Before this course, I used websites and applications without thinking much about where their data is kept. Database Systems showed me that behind many systems there are tables, records, relationships, and queries.
I learned about rows, columns, primary keys, foreign keys, and the connection between different tables. This course felt different from Programming Fundamentals because it focused more on managing information than writing general program logic. However, both courses require careful thinking and accuracy.
Dr. Bilal Ahmad taught us Database Systems and helped us understand that organized data is important in many real-world systems. Hospitals, schools, banks, businesses, and websites all need databases to manage information properly. This made the subject feel practical and useful.
The theory became more interesting when we started working in the lab. We used SQL, WAMPServer, servers, and other practical tools. Database Systems taught me that data is valuable only when it is organized, secure, and easy to access when needed.
