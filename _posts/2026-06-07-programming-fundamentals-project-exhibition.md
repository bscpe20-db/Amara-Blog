The Programming Fundamentals project exhibition was one of the most memorable events of my first semester. Our Heart Attack Risk Prediction project was displayed, and teachers, the Head of Department, and other faculty members visited our table. They asked questions about the dataset, Machine Learning model, graphs, report, and the purpose of the project.
At first, I felt nervous because presenting in front of teachers and faculty members requires confidence. I was worried that I might not be able to explain every part properly. However, once we started presenting, I became more comfortable. We explained how our project used health-related data and how the model could help predict risk.
The exhibition taught me that completing a project is not enough. A student should also be able to explain the project clearly. We had to understand our own work instead of only reading from the report. This improved my speaking skills, teamwork, and confidence.
Under the guidance of Dr. Bilal Ahmad, we learned that projects should have a clear purpose and practical value. The exhibition gave me a chance to see how technical work can be presented professionally. It also motivated me to work more confidently on future projects.
