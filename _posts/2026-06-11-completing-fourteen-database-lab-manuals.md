One of the major parts of our Database Systems course was completing fourteen lab manuals. Each manual included practical tasks related to SQL and database concepts. Some manuals were easier, while others required more time because we had to understand the query, write it correctly, and check the output.
Completing all fourteen manuals taught me the value of consistency. It was not possible to complete the work properly without regular practice. Every manual gave me another opportunity to understand tables, records, keys, queries, and database operations. When I made mistakes, I had to correct them and try again.
The lab manuals also taught me how important documentation is. It was not enough to complete a task; we also had to organize our work and submit it properly. This helped me become more careful with files, screenshots, and written explanations.
By the end of the lab work, I felt more confident in using SQL. I understood that practical learning is different from only reading theory. The manuals made Database Systems more understandable because I could see the result of each command. This experience later helped us in server setup and file-sharing activities.
