My first-semester exams were an important learning experience. The Programming Fundamentals exam was challenging because programming questions require logical thinking under time pressure. I felt nervous before attempting the paper because I knew that I had to understand the logic instead of only remembering definitions.
During the exam, I tried to apply the concepts I had practiced during the semester, especially conditions, loops, and functions. The paper was fair and understandable, which helped me attempt it with more confidence. When the exams were checked, I was happy to receive good marks.
Besides Programming Fundamentals, I also studied ICT, Calculus, and Tarjuma-tul-Quran. Each course contributed to my learning in a different way. Calculus required practice, ICT helped me understand computing concepts, and Tarjuma-tul-Quran added another important academic perspective. However, Programming Fundamentals remained the course that gave me my strongest technical foundation.
This semester taught me that regular practice is better than preparing only before exams. I learned that technical subjects need continuous effort. The experience of my first semester prepared me for the second semester, where I started learning Database Systems.
