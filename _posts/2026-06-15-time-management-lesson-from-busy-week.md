The last week before exams was one of the busiest and most challenging parts of my semester. At that time, we had several pending assignments along with quizzes of Digital Logic Design, Database Systems, and Object-Oriented Programming. Every day felt rushed because we had to reach university early, attend lectures, complete lab work, submit assignments, and prepare for quizzes at the same time. Sometimes we were so busy that we hardly had enough time to eat properly or sit peacefully with our classmates.

The workload felt especially difficult because I had not prepared everything earlier. When different deadlines came together, it became hard to decide which task should be completed first. I tried to manage my work, but the pressure increased because every subject required separate attention. Database Systems needed practice with SQL queries and lab tasks, while other courses had their own assignments and quizzes. This experience made me understand that university work cannot be handled properly only at the end of the semester.

During this busy week, I learned an important lesson about time management. Delaying work may feel easy at first, but it creates much more pressure later. If I prepare lectures, assignments, lab work, and quizzes regularly, I can avoid this kind of stress. I realized that even completing a small part of an assignment every day is better than waiting until the deadline is very close. A simple timetable can make a big difference when there are multiple subjects and tasks.

This experience also taught me that technical knowledge alone is not enough for a Computer Engineering student. Programming, databases, Machine Learning projects, and practical lab work are important, but planning and consistency are equally necessary. In the future, I want to make a better weekly timetable, complete tasks earlier, revise regularly, and keep my notes organized. I also want to give proper time to each course instead of focusing only on urgent deadlines.

Looking back, this semester gave me many valuable experiences, including Programming Fundamentals, project work, exhibitions, SQL, WAMPServer, database lab manuals, server setup, WordPress, and Machine Learning. I also learned how important it is to document my learning journey through my GitHub portfolio. The difficult exam week was tiring, but it gave me a lesson that I will carry into future semesters. I now understand that good preparation, regular effort, and time management can make university life much more manageable.
