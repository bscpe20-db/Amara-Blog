After the first few days, I started understanding that university life requires more responsibility than I expected. There were regular lectures, quizzes, sessions, assignments, and practical tasks. In the beginning, it was difficult for me to manage everything together because every subject had different requirements. Sometimes I had to prepare for a quiz while also completing an assignment or revising a lecture.
I gradually learned that university gives students independence, but this independence comes with responsibility. Teachers guide students, but students have to manage their own time, notes, deadlines, and preparation. I realized that leaving work for the last week creates unnecessary stress. If I complete tasks regularly, it becomes easier to handle the workload.
The teachers were cooperative, and regular lectures helped us remain connected with our courses. I also learned that asking questions is important. At first, I hesitated to ask when I did not understand something, but later I realized that clarification saves time and reduces confusion.
This stage of my journey taught me the importance of planning. It prepared me for technical courses where practice was necessary, especially Programming Fundamentals. My next learning experience was entering the world of coding for the first time.
