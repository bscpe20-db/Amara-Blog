I created this GitHub portfolio to leave my digital footprint as a Computer Engineering student. During every semester, students complete lectures, quizzes, assignments, lab manuals, reports, projects, and presentations. However, after the semester ends, most of this work remains saved in different folders and is often forgotten. I wanted to collect my learning experiences in one place so that I could remember what I learned and see how my skills improve over time.
This idea was suggested by Dr. Bilal Ahmad, our professor at UET Lahore, Faisalabad Campus. He encouraged us to document our work instead of keeping it limited to classroom submissions. I think this practice is useful because a GitHub portfolio can show more than marks. It can show my effort, projects, technical learning, mistakes, and progress as a student.
This portfolio will also help me in the future when I apply for internships, training programs, or jobs. A CV only contains short information, but a portfolio can explain my work in more detail. I can add my Programming Fundamentals project, Database Systems work, reports, screenshots, and reflections. This is the beginning of my journey, and I hope to keep improving this portfolio with every semester.
To understand the professional background that inspired this practice, I also explored [Dr. Bilal Ahmad’s LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/drbilalphd/).
